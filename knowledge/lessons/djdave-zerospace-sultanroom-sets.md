# DJ_Dave — two visualist-collab sets: Zerospace (2022) & The Sultan Room (2021)

Combined SET study of two collaboration models. Neither video has a transcript
and **no code is ever legible** — these are craft studies of how an audio coder
splits a show with a visualist, read entirely from ~65 frames.

- **Zerospace (~32 min, Dec 2022):** two performers on stage *together* inside a
  projection-mapped immersive room — audio at one table, live visuals at the
  other. The visuals are an instrument played in real time.
- **Sultan Room (~47 min, Jul 2021):** the released video IS the graphics — a
  full-length pre-rendered motion-graphics film by Chloe Walecki cut to the set
  audio. The visualist is asynchronous: the collab happened before the show.

## DJ_Dave x Andy Rolfes Live at Zerospace

### Rig / split

- **Room:** Zerospace (Brooklyn) immersive volume — every wall AND the floor is
  a projection/LED surface; the performers stand *inside* the visual, not in
  front of it. Two matching wheeled standing desks, angled toward each other.
- **DJ_Dave (audio, right table):** MacBook + one wide, flat controller with
  backlit pads/knobs (model unresolvable in frames; reads as a
  pad/knob MIDI surface, not decks), headphones on the table. Head-down
  laptop posture most of the set = typing/arranging; hands move to the
  controller at energy peaks.
- **Andy Rolfes (visuals, left table):** a **flight-stick joystick** he rides
  nearly every frame, a compact 25-key mini MIDI keyboard (MiniLab-style,
  pads + keys), and small interface boxes. Joystick = continuous camera /
  deformation flight through the 3D scene; keyboard pads = scene/parameter
  triggers. Software not on camera; behavior (realtime 3D, audio-reactive
  layers, live glitch) is TouchDesigner/Notch-class — flagged as inference.
- **Coupling:** visuals are audio-reactive (a pink spectrum/waveform bar wall
  appears at ~2:40) and code-aware — near the close, syntax-colored **source
  code is projected across the whole wall** (31:20). The audio laptop's editor
  is being fed to the visual rig as a mixable texture layer.

### Set timeline

two captures: a clean "inside the volume" multicam (no crowd visible) and a
from-the-crowd wide shot of the same wall.

### Transition mechanics

- **Visual eras ARE the arrangement.** Every 2–4 minutes the room hard-swaps
  world (palette + geometry family). Song/section boundaries in the audio are
  legible from stills alone because the visualist cuts scenes at them — the
  collab contract is "I change worlds when you change songs."
- **Contrast cycling:** white-out room → saturated single-hue room → near-black
  room, round and round. Brightness of the *entire venue* is used the way a
  solo coder uses a filter sweep: it resets the crowd's eyes before each build.
- **The joystick is a transition instrument.** Rolfes rides camera flight
  through the 3D scene continuously, so even static audio plateaus have motion;
  at drops he can whip the whole room's perspective. Continuous manual motion
  glues over the audio's discrete edits.
- **Audio-reactive layers carry micro-transitions** (waveform walls, ink
  splats, digit rain popping on hits) while scene swaps carry macro-transitions.
- **Trough staging:** the darkest, emptiest room (~16:40) sits mid-set —
  classic energy-arc valley before the chevron peak era.
- **Code-as-final-visual:** ending on the projected editor is both a reveal
  and a credential — the transition out of the show is into the *making* of it.

### Techniques

- **Two-table duet staging** — audio and visuals as symmetric instruments:
  matching desks, matching yellow outfits, both visibly *playing* all set. The
  visualist is presented as a bandmate, not a booth tech.
- **Perform inside the render** — projection-mapped floor + walls put the
  performers' silhouettes into the piece; camera crews shoot through the
  volume so every angle is composed.
- **World-swap = section marker** — discrete scene changes at musical
  boundaries; within a world, only parameters (color, density, camera) evolve.
- **Physical macro controller for visuals** — a joystick gives the visualist
  the same "one expressive knob" the audio coder gets from a slider: cheap,
  continuous, watchable.
- **Editor as texture** — pipe the live-code editor into the visual mix as a
  layer you can bring up at will (here: saved for the closing reveal).
- **Two-capture edit** — shoot a clean in-volume pass and a crowd pass, and
  intercut; the crowd shots authenticate the show, the clean shots flatter the
  graphics.

## DJ_Dave Set at The Sultan Room (Graphics by Chloe Walecki)

### Rig / split

- The published video contains **zero stage footage** — 47 minutes of
  full-frame motion graphics over the set audio. The performance rig is
  invisible; era (Jul 2021) puts her in her **Sonic Pi** period (inference —
  her GitHub Universe 2020 and "Easy" sets were Sonic Pi; Strudel arrives in
  her 2022 material).
- **Chloe Walecki (visuals)** works *offline*: the film is polished 3D motion
  graphics — flat solid-color backgrounds, extruded nested wireframe
  primitives (rings, stars, rectangles, triangles, hearts) with soft contact
  shadows and film-grain particles — Cinema 4D-class render quality, not
  realtime (inference from shading/DOF/grain).
- So the collab split here is **temporal**, not spatial: the audio coder plays
  the room; the visualist authors a synced film that becomes the archival/
  broadcast identity of the set.

### Set timeline

Motif + palette per sweep frame (every ~2:20). The film's grammar: ONE
primitive family per section, hard palette swaps between sections, grey
"static" interludes as breakdowns.

- **[0:00] Black open.**
- **[0:20–2:20] Ring era, magenta.** Coral concentric rings orbiting on a
  magenta field; by 2:20 the field bleaches to lavender with magenta glitch
  tearing at the edges — intro building to first groove.
- **[4:40] Star era, teal.** Nested five-point star outlines tunnel on dark
  purple.
- **[6:00] Grey interlude #1.** Flat charcoal field, dust/particle specks,
  blue glitch fringes — visual near-silence; reads as a breakdown or talk gap.
- **[7:00] Ring era, red-on-red.** Tone-on-tone red ellipses — low-contrast =
  simmering groove.
- **[9:20] Grey interlude #2.**
- **[10:20] Ring era, purple + peach.** High-contrast ring tunnel — energy up.
- **[11:40] Circuit era, magenta + cyan.** Angular zig-zag extrusions like
  circuit traces — the most "electronic" motif; midset drive.
- **[14:00] Heart era, greyscale.** Nested wireframe hearts, monochrome with
  grain — a sentimental/ballad-coded section (she has love-song material;
  motif tracks mood).
- **[15:20–16:20] Recap run: magenta rings → teal stars.** Earlier motifs
  return — visual "reprise" suggesting recurring musical themes or a
  chorus-family of songs.
- **[18:40–22:00] Red era.** Red rings at 18:40 and again 22:00 bracketing
  grey interlude #3 (21:00) — long high-energy stretch with one breather.
- **[23:20] Rect era, green + yellow.** Nested warped rectangles on bright
  green — sharpest palette turn in the film (hot green after all that
  red/magenta) — clear act break, second half begins.
- **[25:40] Ring era, orange + pink.**
- **[28:00] Triangle era, blue + red.** Nested triangles, primary-color clash —
  peak aggression motif.
- **[30:20] Ring era, purple + peach (return).**
- **[32:40] Stripe era, slate blue.** Vertical/diagonal line bundles,
  low-saturation — cooldown.
- **[35:00] Rect era, acid green (return, denser).**
- **[37:20] Ring era, lime + magenta.** Loudest complementary-clash palette —
  late-set second peak.
- **[39:40] Wire-bow era, greyscale.** Twisted looped-wire knot (bow /
  infinity form) on grey — second sentimental valley.
- **[41:00–42:00] Datamosh finale ramp.** Acid yellow + magenta smeared
  glitch/datamosh of the ring motif, then green/magenta/orange star-field
  glitch — the film starts destroying its own motifs; reads as the "everything
  at once" closer.
- **[44:20] Wire-bow reprise, greyscale.**
- **[46:40] Rect/pentagon era, two-tone green — end.** Closes calm, on a
  clean motif, not a blackout.

### Transition mechanics

- **Motif-per-section:** each musical section owns one primitive family; the
  cut between families is the transition. Within a section, only camera slide,
  ring spacing, and shadow drift evolve — cheap sustained motion, exactly like
  holding a groove.
- **Palette as energy dial:** tone-on-tone (red/red) = simmer; complementary
  clash (lime/magenta, blue/red) = peak; greyscale = emotional valley. The
  film's saturation curve IS the set's energy curve.
- **Grey static interludes as breakdowns:** flat grain fields with glitch
  fringes between eras — visual silence matching musical strip-downs; they
  recur at ~6:00, 9:20, 21:00.
- **Reprise structure:** motifs return in later palettes (stars, rects, rings,
  wire-bows each appear 2–3 times) — the film mirrors an original-music set
  where hooks and themes recur, rather than a DJ set's one-way tracklist.
- **Glitch = climax, not error:** datamosh smears are saved for the final ramp
  (41:00–42:00), destroying established motifs for the ending — the visual
  equivalent of a filter-wrecked last drop.

### Techniques

- **Asynchronous visualist collab** — commission a motion designer to score
  your recorded set with a film keyed to its structure; the show gets a second
  life as a watchable artifact even when the live room had no projection
  budget.
- **One-primitive discipline** — a whole section built from a single nested
  outline shape; identity comes from palette + motion, so sections stay
  instantly distinguishable at a glance (and cheap to author).
- **Flat-color + contact-shadow look** — solid background, extruded splines,
  soft shadows, grain: a strong signature style that survives compression and
  reads on any screen size.
- **Saturation arc planning** — map songs to palettes before rendering so the
  whole 47 minutes has a deliberate contrast curve (clash at peaks, greyscale
  at valleys, acid clash + glitch at the finale).
- **Motif reprise** — reuse a section's shape family when its musical theme
  returns; the audience learns the mapping without being told.

## Vocabulary

- **immersive volume** — a room where walls + floor are all display surface
  (Zerospace); performers stand inside the graphics rather than in front of a
  backdrop.
- **world-swap** — hard scene change of the entire visual environment at a
  musical section boundary; the macro-transition unit of a visualist set.
- **era / motif section** — a stretch of set owned by one visual family
  (palette + primitive); the still-frame-legible signature of a song section.
- **contrast cycling** — alternating white-out, saturated, and near-black
  rooms to reset the audience's eyes between builds (venue-scale filter sweep).
- **audio-reactive layer** — visual element driven directly by the audio
  (waveform walls, ink splats on hits); carries micro-transitions between
  world-swaps.
- **joystick camera flight** — visualist performance gesture: continuous
  manual navigation through the 3D scene, keeping motion alive during musical
  plateaus and whipping perspective at drops.
- **editor-as-texture / code reveal** — feeding the live-code editor into the
  visual mix as a layer; DJ_Dave's sets close on it as a "this was all code"
  signature.
- **two-capture edit** — intercutting a clean in-volume pass with a
  from-the-crowd pass in the published video.
- **asynchronous visualist** — a graphics collaborator who scores the set
  offline (Walecki) versus a live visualist playing alongside (Rolfes); the
  two collab models of this lesson.
- **one-primitive discipline** — building an entire visual section from a
  single nested shape family, differentiated only by palette and motion.
- **grey interlude** — flat grain/static field between motif eras; visual
  breakdown marking a musical strip-down or reset.
- **saturation arc** — the planned contrast curve of palettes across a set:
  tone-on-tone simmer → complementary clash peaks → greyscale valleys →
  glitch/datamosh finale.
- **motif reprise** — bringing back an earlier section's shape family in a new
  palette when its musical theme returns.
- **datamosh finale** — smearing/corrupting established motifs as the closing
  climax; glitch reserved as a destination, not a garnish.
